1. NLP Basics
Natural Language Processing (NLP) allows LLMs to analyze, interpret, and generate human-

2. Tokenization
Text is divided into smaller pieces (tokens), which can be words, subwords, or even
characters. This helps LLMs process information efficiently.
Continue Learning
3. Word Prediction
LLMs predict the most probable next word in a sentence using vast amounts of training
data, ensuring coherent and contextually accurate responses.
Continue Learning
4. Role of Data
LLMs learn from books, articles, conversations, and websites to develop a broad
understanding of human language and improve response accuracy.
Continue Learning
5. Hallucination Issue
Sometimes, LLMs generate incorrect or misleading information because they rely on
patterns rather than factual understanding.
Continue Learning
6. Future Potential
Instead of truly understanding text, LLMs recognize statistical relationships between words
to generate responses that appear human-like.
